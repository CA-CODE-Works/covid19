All Californians—regardless of where they live, their working environment, their social supports, or how they identify⁠—deserve a healthy life.

COVID-19 has highlighted existing inequities in health. Committed to a California for All, the state is identifying communities most impacted and directing resources to address COVID-19 health inequities. Reducing COVID-19 risk in all communities is good for everyone, and California is committed to making it part of our reopening plan.

State public health leaders cannot address COVID-19 health inequities alone. A healthy California for everyone requires partnership with the private sector, local government, and community partners at all levels.

The disparities in our diverse communities are severe

COVID-19 disproportionately affects California’s low income, Latino, Black, Pacific Islander, and essential workers such as those in health care, grocery, and cleaning services.

Note: Case rate is defined as cumulative COVID-19 cases per 100K population. Death rate is defined as cumulative COVID-19 deaths per 100K.

Reopening equitably

To ensure we build a California for All, counties must address COVID-19 health inequities in their economic reopening plans. Each week, state officials work with local health departments to measure case rates and positivity rates, as well as positivity rates in the county’s most disproportionately-impacted neighborhoods (health equity metric).

As part of this broad effort, counties must:

  • Define their disproportionately impacted populations and measure COVID-19 metrics and response (contract tracing, isolation support, worker protections) in these populations
  • Show how they will use state funds to support disproportionately-impacted communities
  • Show that the health equity metric does not significantly lag behind its overall county positivity rate
