Californians need safe and reliable childcare options during the coronavirus (COVID-19) response. MyChildCare.ca.gov provides an interactive map of qualified licensed childcare providers near you. The tool is available in English and Spanish.

To find childcare 

  1. Go to MyChildCare.ca.gov.
  2. Search by location, health and safety details, ages of children, capacity, and hours of care.

If you have questions about the provider, contact the California Child Care Resource & Referral Network. They can help you understand childcare options and how to access childcare subsidies. 

To add your childcare service to the provider list 

If you are a licensed childcare provider, your work is essential. Contact the California Child Care Resource & Referral Network to update your hours of operation and availability. 

More resources

  • For general childcare questions (available in English or Spanish), call 1-800-KIDS-793
  • Providers can find more information at the California Department of Social Services and the California Department of Education

Questions and answers

What if a provider says they have availability on MyChildCare.ca.gov but when I connect with them they have no space?

The information on MyChildCare.ca.gov will be updated twice each week but it is possible that vacancies are filled between updates. Contact the California Child Care Resource & Referral Network to find more vacancies. 

How can I find out more about the provider listed in MyChildCare.ca.gov?

MyChildCare.ca.gov includes each provider’s facility/license number. This number can be searched at the California Department of Social Service’s Community Care Licensing Database to find more information about the provider, like information on inspections and complaints.  

How can I find help paying for childcare?

MyChildCare.ca.gov provides information on all licensed center-based and family childcare homes. This includes both private-pay and subsidized childcare. This is subject to capacity and eligibility requirements. The California Child Care Resource & Referral Network can answer more questions about childcare subsidies.

Can I get childcare during the stay home order?

Yes, all childcare facilities can open with necessary modifications.

Can my babysitter still come to the house?

Yes, a childcare worker can come to your home if necessary and should practice basic prevention guidelines (like hand washing for at least 20 seconds, physical distancing, and staying home if feeling ill).
