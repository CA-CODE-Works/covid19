The Governor’s Task Force on Business & Jobs Recovery identified support for educators as a critical factor for California’s recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic. For schools that have not reopened for in-person instruction, educators continue to navigate the challenges of providing high quality distance learning. To help teachers, as well as parents and others supporting students in remote education environments, the Task Force has provided the following list of vetted resources that offer training to teach remotely. There are many distance learning resources available and the following list was selected for their quality and scope.

Additional resources may be added to this page as needed.

San José State University Online Teacher Academy

The SJSU Lurie College of Education K-12 Online Teaching Academy was established in Summer 2020 in response to the inequities in learning exacerbated by the COVID-19 crisis and was intended to create free opportunities for pre-service teacher candidates and current teachers to strengthen their online skills. The 23 Summer 2020 webinar recordings feature a wide range of teachers, professors, and other practitioners who discuss how to build equity, employ emancipatory pedagogies, utilize a multitude of online platforms, and more. Lurie College is organizing additional webinars for January and February 2021.

View Webinar Series

UC San Diego Teaching Online Essentials

UC San Diego Extension has offered a certificate program in online teaching for more than 15 years. When the pandemic struck, Extension’s Education and Community Outreach (ECO) Department distilled this proven curriculum into an intensive training experience called Teaching Online Essentials.

Teachers and Parents can find best practices in online and hybrid education, emphasizing creativity, connectivity and transformative experiences geared to the needs of diverse learners.

CTA, Stanford SCOPE and NBRC Instructional Leadership Corps

The California Teachers Association (CTA), Stanford Center for Opportunity Policy in Education (SCOPE) and the National Board Resource Center (NBRC) educator-led Instructional Leadership Corps (ILC) grows the ability of local educators to enrich instruction and assessment practices in their schools by creating professional learning experiences and resources for other educators.

Access a variety of resources including the ILC Professional Learning Handbook, Teacher Tools for Practice, and member-led distance learning webinars.

CTA Instruction and Professional Development

The California Teachers Association (CTA) Instruction and Professional Development (IPD) Team connects 310,000 CTA members with technical assistance, professional learning, resources, and networks to help them grow and thrive as professional educators in strong public schools.

Access professional learning sessions led by current practitioners focused on effective practices for engagement, instruction, and assessment for diverse learners in the context of distance and hybrid learning.

Access professional learning opportunities and available resources.

Google Teacher Center

Google for Education’s Teacher Center puts the power and resources of Google at educators’ fingertips around the globe. Through curated trainings, classroom resources, professional development, and connective communities, educators can transform the learning environment with the students and supplement their pedagogical expertise.

Basic and advanced skills across Google tools

No log-in necessary to access content, but recommended to track progress. Google Certified Educator exams require payment to obtain credentials and take the exam (recommended through Kryterion)

Apple Education Resources

Apple provides a variety of programs to support education, such as Apple Teacher, a self-paced online professional learning program to support and celebrate educators using Apple products for teaching and learning. To support the transition to an online learning environment, Apple Education created the Apple Education Learning Series (AELS) on-demand videos, as well as weekly virtual conferences led by Apple Professional Learning Specialists to discuss the video content with educators. Apple Professional Learning Specialists can also provide a one-to-one, 30 minute, virtual coaching session to support personal learning.

Build skills on iPad and Mac that directly apply to activities with students
