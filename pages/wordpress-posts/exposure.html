Steps after notification

If you were notified of possible COVID-19 exposure via the California COVID Notify app, please do the following:

If you’re at UCSD

  • Go to [link] to find out what to do next.
  • Quarantine to protect others. Learn how.
  • Check UCSD’s COVID-19 policies and guidance

If you’re at UCSF

  • Call (415) 514-7666 to find out what to do next.
  • Quarantine to protect others. Learn how.
  • Check UCSF’s COVID-19 policies and guidance

If you have COVID-19 symptoms, such as fever, cough, trouble breathing, or loss of taste or smell, self-isolate and seek medical attention immediately. Check your symptoms.

We know this is stressful

If you are anxious, stressed or scared because of this notification, you are not alone. Fear and anxiety about a new disease and what could happen can be overwhelming and cause strong emotions. It is important that you first try to talk to your campus COVID team to assess your risk, discuss testing and treatment options, and seek emotional support. If for any reason you are unable to talk to someone from your campus team immediately, here is some support you until you can. 

  • Watch this CDC video about managing anxiety and stress 
  • See this list of resources if you feel like you need to talk to someone and want emotional support
  • Read these playbooks for managing stress from the California Surgeon General

About the app

California COVID Notify quickly notifies you if you may have been exposed to COVID-19. You can then protect your friends and family, reducing the spread of infection in your community.

The State of California is partnering with the University of California to test the app in a limited pilot. The results will determine whether the app will be used among all Californians.

Thanks for your help in keeping our communities safe.
